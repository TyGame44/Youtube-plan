# Quantum physics is everywhere



Quantum physics is a theory based on sampling and probability distributions.

This is because when you take a snapshot of a system, you only get the instantaneous data about the measurement.



In quantum physics, this limits us to only measuring an atom's position or momentum. We can trade accuracy in one for accuracy in another but we can never know both.



In real life we see sampling like this all of the time.



Have you ever noticed how car tires seem to start spinning backwards.



This is caused by the tire speed vs the sampling rate. And what is causing the sampling rate. It is the streetlights and headlights all around you.



They are either attached to the AC current of the city or they are attached to a pulse width modulation circuit to adjust their brightness.



In both cases they rapidly flash on and off. So you are sampling the tires 60 times per second. If the rotations per second is 60 or 30 or 15 then the tire looks like it isn’t moving. If it's slightly above or below one of those values. The tires look like they are slowly rotating forwards or backwards.



You can also notice this effect with fidget spinners and any modern screen. Modern screens insert a full black image between every color image. This is to reduce motion blur on the screen.



This same effect causes a flashing light that samples the fidget spinner either 60 or 30 times per second.



So based on the ratio of the fidget spinner's speed to the refresh rate of the tv, the fidget spinner may appear to stop moving, or start spinning forwards or backwards.
