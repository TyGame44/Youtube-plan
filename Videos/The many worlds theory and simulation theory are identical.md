# The many worlds theory and simulation theory are identical

The only conclusion you could draw from simulation theory is, unless you’re noticing something actively adversarial, that your actions if you were in a simulation would be identical to if you were not in a simulation. Why would you change how you behave because there’s a risk of you being simulated? That's weird behavior.



If the many world lines interpretation of quantum physics is true. You can draw a philosophical equality behind the interpretations of a simulation, being restarted, and different timelines where you live in one timeline dying in another.
