# Why are octopus smart

Are octopuses smart because of their camouflage?

Recent discoveries in mitochondria Quantum neurofilament and mitochondria light based energy control feedback systems lead me to this conclusion.

In the brain mitochondria communicate to each other using light to manage energy production.

I propose that octopuses are uniquely intelligent because they can intentionally limit or restrict what and where information gets shared within the brain . They can partially turn it off and they can likely tunnel information to specific parts so a mitochondria control signal could be focused.

My proposed structure of an octopus brain would have a rotational light blocking structure so that it can delegate multiple tasks to each arm then process the tasks individually. This would give octopuses extraordinary multitasking problem solving abilities.
