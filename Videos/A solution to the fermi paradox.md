# A solution to the fermi paradox

The fermi paradox asks why haven't we seen aliens. I propose our surroundings already answer that question.

We are in a very interesting part of the milky way.



We are located in between two spirals of much denser stars.



We are uniquely positioned so we are close enough to the galactic center to have all of the more complex elements that are formed under more extreme conditions.



But we are also in one of the coldest areas of the milky way that has these materials.



If we were much closer to either of the spirals on either side of us, it would likely be too hot for life to form.



Interestingly, this gives us another site to look for life in the milky way. There is one similar spot on the other side of the milky way that would have the same elements and temperature.



But as far as the fermi-paradox goes, this goes fairly far in explaining why we haven’t encountered alien life within the milky way.
