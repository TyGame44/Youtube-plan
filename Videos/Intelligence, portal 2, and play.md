# Intelligence, portal 2, and play



Recent data has shown that you can learn 20 times faster when you are playing.



Even if you learn something it doesn’t necessarily mean an increase in IQ score.



Portal 2 is unique in that it is the only experience we have identified that measurably increases your IQ score before you play to after.



I want to propose that portal 2 teaches fundamental aspects of discontinuous spaces. This gives you an intuitive understanding of a whole set of equations you rarely experience in the natural world.



I propose 2 games, both designed around using play to teach about physics.



I am working on a game where you race other players to the speed of light. This lets you experience all of the general and special relativity effects in a fun and engaging way.



You can naturally learn about these concepts. I also propose that this game specifically would have a similar effect on IQ as portal 2. Portal 2 teaches about discontinuous spaces and this game would teach about hyperbolic spaces.



The second game idea would have a magic system based on quantum physics. This would give players an intuitive understanding of all of the weirdness of quantum physics.



I think the IQ increase argument is more difficult for this game idea. But it could still teach about probability spaces and increase IQ in that regard.



Education makes us smart. Play makes us genius.
