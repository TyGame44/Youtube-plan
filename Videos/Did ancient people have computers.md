# Did ancient people have computers?



The ancients are documented to heavily rely on water for mechanics. This is heavily documented.



The ancient Romans used Ruina montium, Wrecking of mountains, to mine metals. This technique used pressurized water to shatter rocks and demolish mountains.



I want to propose that the spiral structures under the pyramids exist to use water pressure to lift the giant stone blocks through a central column.



This is because the spiral structures could be used to pressurize a large column of water that connects to a spiral channel around the column. This creates a high pressure water that could lift the giant blocks on the pyramid. This makes sense because a complex structure under the pyramid probably contributed to its creation.



I also want to propose that Stepwells were used as the first computers. You could create a series of siphons to perform logical operations with traditional pottery. The design of the stairs make it look like they are optimized for branching logic and more complex operations.
