# How might you collect real-time brain data



MRI (Magnetic resonance imaging) provides us with nearly perfect highly detailed scans of the brain. The problem is that these devices take up entire rooms.





I propose a simplification of the MRI for portable, non-invasive, real time brain scans.

The trick here will be to target specific molecules for magnetic resonance rather than testing all the frequency ranges. We could either target specific neurotransmitters to monitor brain spiking or target a specific molecule that we introduce with an mRNA virus that makes the body naturally produce polarized chemicals in the brain that let us map the brain activity with the same type of targeted MRI.
