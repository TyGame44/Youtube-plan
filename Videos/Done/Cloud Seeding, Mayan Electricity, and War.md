# Cloud Seeding, Mayan Electricity, and War

In 1915, the state of California was going through an extended drought and hired one Charles Hatfield to produce rain. Charles Hatfield built a large tower and released a secret chemical into the air. Soon after, massive rains arrived that caused catastrophic floods and destroyed dams.


In the 1940-50s, the science behind cloudseeding was discovered. We discovered that you can use silver iodide to seed clouds.


Other discovered methods of cloud seeding include delivering an electric charge into the sky. If that works I wonder about the reverse. Could removing the electric potential from the sky reduce rainfall and cause droughts.


There is evidence of ancient people first using electricity by harnessing the atmosphere voltage at different elevations. There is some evidence of this in both the pyramids in Egypt and the mayan pyramids. I would like to lay out the theory that these devices may have caused regional droughts and thus delayed our understanding of electricity. We may have developed mysticism about the dangers of electricity if it caused droughts.


In 2021, science collected the first real world data on cloud seedings' direct impact on climate change. This was because of covid. Covid-19 significantly reduced our carbon emissions and had a measurable delay on climate change. But despite this, the world got warmer than expected over this period. It was discovered that shipping vessels burning very crude oil were unintentionally cloud seeding over the ocean by releasing silver iodine and other salts.


The heat reflected by these clouds helped to hide the extent of global warming. When we lost the cloud coverage from these shipping containers. We noticed a 0.05C - 0.1C degree change. Global temperatures as a whole have risen 1.5C from global warming so these shipping vessels could be masking 7% of global warming.
