# This is how we solve Microplastics

This is quite a shocking statistic. Did you know that 78% of the microplastics in the ocean come from synthetic tire rubber.


Let me explain. If we just look at plastic by mass, 75% of it is fishing gear like nets. But recent findings have shown that these larger plastics eventually settle to the bottom of the ocean and become sediment.


So then if we actually look at the source for microplastics by count. We find that almost 80% of microplastic comes from car tires.


This problem is exacerbated as electric cars become more common. An electric car is heavier than a traditional car and thus produces more tire pollution.


So I want to propose a solution.


Almost all of the rubber microplastic is generated on highways.


So I want to slightly modify semi trucks to collect tire pollution.


I want to build a device that can be mounted underneath the bottom of trucks and would use the airflow of the truck to collect tire pollution and remove it from the environment.


The device would use the momentum / airflow underneath the trucks to generate vortexes.


These vortexes would be engineered and optimized to pick up tire microplastics and direct them into a filter. The filter could also use the momentum of the air to separate out the particles.


The goal for this is to have no moving parts on the filter and have a minimal/zero impact on the truck's efficiency.


I want to target semi trucks because they spend the most time on the highways, they create the most tire pollution, and a small change to a few of these trucks would have a disproportionately positive outcome on reducing tire pollution.


We could easily eliminate 75% of the microplastic exposure to ourselves and our environment with only a cheap small scale rollout of tire airflow filters.
