# Whisps, Spinners, and Mil-Orbs


Light weight devices could remain suspended in the air and be maneuverable by EMF frequencies. I want to propose a technology, discuss how it may be developed, discuss how it may have advanced, and talk about potential use cases.



I propose a chain of atoms that carries spaced positive and negative charges. You could align it with an electromagnetic frequency and accelerate it. 



This technology could begin with genetically modified tree seeds. These lightweight fluffy seeds would be the perfect candidate for early generation whisps. You could maneuver them to a location, then have them potentially stream back audio.



As protein folding evolves, I propose many lighter weight variations of this technology. You could shrink down the emf maneuverable audio devices. You could make photoreactive proteins and have them record videos.



At this point, the technology evolves into highly maneuverable “spinners”. These would instead use emf to transfer a rotational force and allow for atmospheric propulsion. 



This leads to the last variation, Mil-Orbs. I propose that these are anti-radar technology. By using the inverse square law in combination with proteins that ride that frequency back to its source, you could release a swarm of Mil-Orbs that by design would automatically swarm active radar systems.



I also propose that whisps / Mil-Orbs could be used to help establish sites on the moon, mars, and asteroids. These ultra-light weight packages address a major problem of the rocket equation. Optimizing mass has always been a goal in rocket launches but this would enable an entirely new scale of space exploration. We could prep landing sites with mil-orbs or potentially use them to mine asteroids.
