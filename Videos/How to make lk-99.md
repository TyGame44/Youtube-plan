# How to make lk-99

lk 99 is a proposed superconductor, but has been very difficult to manufacture due to its very specific crystalline structure

Several labs have confirmed this crystalline structure by verifying it’s one way superconductivity versus the traditional, two way superconductivity



Simulation results also confirm this one way superconducting property



I propose that this crystal structure could be created very expensively with our current EUV lithography techniques.



The steps would be as follows

Sputter one layer of one element, photo mask that layer, etch, wash, 

Now this is an interesting one. I propose a sputtering/grinding stage at this point.



You would sputter on another element then you would grind it down.

This would theoretically create a layer of the ideal crystallized structure. Or at least enough of it to get the super conducting properties.



You would repeat this process to build up the crystalline structure staged by stage. A photo step might be necessary to examine the current crystal formations.

If the crystals are large enough, this might be easy.



I propose a second technique for creating these structures with many much less steps. What if we flipped the status quo currently we aim to produce near vacuum conditions for UV lithography.



What if instead we tried ultra dense atmospheres for EUV lithography. We could create ultra dense ultra precise plasma on the surface of the chip. This could potentially reduce this production to two steps.



One sputtering/spin step and one ultra dense plasma EUV lithography step. 



that would be cool to create a lk-99. I would love to attempt the second idea to cheaply produce LK 99 at scale.
