# Religion and Halos



We’ve recently discovered that humans glow. This is specifically related to mitochondrial energy, so energy processing. We know the brain uses most of the energy, so it would make sense that the brain glows more than the rest of the body.



I wonder if in deep dark caves you might be able to see the faint glow of people. This could help understand why so many ancient cultures had cultural traditions about caves.



We also know that due to atmospheric effects on mountains that you can see halos around peoples heads. This is just an optical illusion caused by the atmosphere.



Imagine, combining these now to form the basis of religion. The Greeks in Romans would’ve had a well established Olympus and Hades. They would be aware that people glow and you can see that glow in caves. They would also have noticed this glow on mountain tops.



You could make a religion out of this.



Christianity seems to have taken it to the obvious next step with halos. You’re on mountain tops and caves, two traditionally mystical places you would see Halo’s glowing around people’s heads.
