# Were Renaissance Orchestras the first computers



I don’t have any evidence that this is the case but wouldn’t it be beautiful if we could convert an entire orchestra into a computer.



The three body problem has a really cool scene where thousands of people are raising and lowering flags to act as binary operations in a computer.



What if we applied this concept to an orchestra? We could potentially need much fewer people to do the same calculations.



We would have an encoding step where each player was given a music sheet based on the program we would want to run, then we would listen for harmonies and dysharmonies in the music to get the results.



We could chain together multiple simple programs like this to do complex calculations. If we prepared different music sheets based on the program we wanted to run and had quick intervals between deciding your sheet music based on the results and playing the next program.
