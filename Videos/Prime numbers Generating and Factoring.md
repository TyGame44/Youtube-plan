# Prime numbers: Generating and Factoring



1 is novel. 2 is novel. 3 is novel because it combines 1 and 2. 4 is not novel because it is the same as 2. 5 is novel because 2 + 3 = 5. 6 is not novel because of both 2 and 3.



This sounds simple but I believe that this idea can unravel the reinman hypothesis, identify how n + 1 is equivalent to the ½ line and address how an infinite sum compares to the prime number wheel generation.



Let's start by sequentially multiplying the primes together and examining how we form a wheel with this process that places primes as twin coordinates around this circle.



So we can find that the product of n primes has roughly 2x that values predictive power.



For example, 2*3 = 6



6 is not prime but 7 and 5 are. Ie +- 1. 

30 is not prime but 30 +- 1, +- 7, +- 11, +- 13



This mostly holds true but we find a few exceptions.

So we have to filter out wrong answers either in later passes or by accounting for them using the primes between the prime product and primes above the primes used but less than the product value.



This could be used to create a quantum computer for factoring. You might not even need to remove the erroneous predictions because they would still produce correct factoring results.



This type of system would account for small primes (Still very large numbers). But if you wanted you could combine it with traditional database technology that would have a reference of very large primes. So you would have a 2 sided approach to account for small primes and large primes when factoring.



I wonder if this idea really adds anything to the reinman hypothesis. It is already very close to wheel factorization and the sieve of atkins. I just wonder if it could be better used as a selector rather than a filter.
