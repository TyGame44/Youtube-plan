## AI Bias and the meme that caused the war



It sounds crazy to say that a meme may have been the direct cause or played a significant role in these atrocities.



### “Bomb bomb bomb, Bomb Iran, Bomb bomb bomb, Bomb Iran, Bomb Iran, Bomb Iran”



When training an AI model, you scrape the entire internet. You deduplicate content and you filter out AI generated content.







Memes in general would find themselves overly influential on the models because they are mass created by real people and include many variations. This makes them especially problematic for basic/naive content filtering.







This creates the perfect storm for the bomb iran meme to be transcribed and fed into an LLM along with many different video prompts and descriptions. This large variety of data sends a strong signal to the AI to bomb Iran in a wide range of contexts. The model isn’t just biased towards bombing Iran. But it is told that that is the best course of action in a plethora of military situations.







If Trump asked an AI if he should invade Iran. This meme would heavily bias the model towards war.



If the AI is being used to decide on targets and it has an underlying bias towards wanting to bomb Iran then it is much more likely to bomb a school.







This is doubled down upon if any of the models have reasoning turned on. As they all likely do. Apple research into oneshot models vs reasoning models have shown that any reasoning perpetuates minor training biases into major biases in the output.
